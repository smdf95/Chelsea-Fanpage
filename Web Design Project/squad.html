Squad 2022/2023

Defenders

Thiago Silva

Silva has been colossol for Chelsea since joining. 2022/23 has been no different. Silva has consistently been Chelsea's best defender this season, and his absence is always noticed.

Kepa Arrizabillaga

Kepa has come a long long way from his form under Lampard in 2019/2020. This season has seen a complete revival of Kepa's career, being responsible for saving Chelsea points across several games.

Ben Chilwell

Chilwell has spent a lot of this season on the sidelines after sustaining a bad hamstring injury in a group stage game against RB Salzburg. Chilwell's absence was painfully noticed, as his counterpart Cucurella had a debut season from hell. Chilwell has come up at big moments when he plays and scored vital goals.

Midifelders

Enzo Fernandez

Despite only joining in January, Enzo has been a vital addition to Chelsea's midfield. A certain upgrade on Jorginho, who was often criticised for passing sideways and backwards too often. Enzo has the passing range and vision to pick passes behind defences with ease. While the â‚¬120m price tag is certainly an outrageous price to pay for anyone, Enzo looks as thought he might just live up to the price.

Ngolo Kante

Kante's absence has certainly been missed. Kante is essential to the way Chelsea play, regardless of the manager in charge. Kante's performances when available have been incredible, especially considering his long-term injury that kept him on the sidelines for the majority of the season.

Attackers

Kai Havertz

Despite a season of overall poor performances, Kai Havertz is statistically Chelsea's best attacker. It's important to note that that is a particularly low bar. 10 goal contributions across 40+ games is an abysmal record for a club's top attacker. Despite the embarassment of riches for Chelsea's attack, the quality is so incredibly poor, that the only player to make the list of top attackers is Havertz because he statistically has to be. Here's hoping to a new striker next season.
